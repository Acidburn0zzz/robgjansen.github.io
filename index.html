I Am Co-chairing WPES 2015

Sunday, May 17th, 2015 | Comments

I am co-chairing the 2015 Workshop on Privacy in the Electronic Society. The call for papers is currently available on the workshop website, and papers can be submitted through the submission site. Please consider submitting and join a discussion with the top minds in digital privacy.

WPES is co-located with the 22nd ACM Conference on Computer and Communications Security (CCS), which will take place in Denver, Colorado on October 12-16, 2015.

The Sniper Attack Won the NRL Berman Award

Sunday, May 17th, 2015 | Comments

Our paper entitled “The Sniper Attack: Anonymously Deanonymizing and Disabling the Tor Network” won the 47th Annual Alan Berman Research Publication Award! This award was given by the U.S. Naval Research Laboratory on 2015-04-17 for best paper in the Information Technology Division.

Paper Accepted to WPES 2014

Sunday, September 14th, 2014 | Comments

Our paper entitled “IMUX: Managing Tor Connections from Two to Infinity, and Beyond” has been accepted to WPES 2014!

Blog Post Summarizing New Tor+Bitcoin Research

Monday, July 14th, 2014 | Comments

I just published a new post on the TorProject blog summarizing the current state of research on Tor incentive schemes and related challenges. The post includes information about the new TorCoin and TEARS research papers that will appear at HotPETs later this week.

Paper Accepted to USENIX Security 2014

Tuesday, May 27th, 2014 | Comments

Our paper entitled “Never Been KIST: Tor’s Congestion Management Blossoms with Kernel-Informed Socket Transport” has been conditionally accepted upon shepherd approval accepted to USENIX Security 2014!

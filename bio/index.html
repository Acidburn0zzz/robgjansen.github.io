Bio

I am a computer scientist in the Center for High Assurance Computer Systems at the U.S. Naval Research Laboratory where I work most closely with Aaron Johnson and Paul Syverson. I work on computer and network security, and am a self-proclaimed security experimentalist because I consider myself as “one who prefers to ascertain by finding out.”

I was awarded a PhD from the Computer Science Department at the University of Minnesota in October, 2012. I have had the great honor of being advised by Nicholas Hopper while also working closely with Yongdae Kim and others from the distributed systems security lab.

My research interests lie in distributed system security, peer-to-peer systems, network and protocol security, anonymous communication and privacy, and network simulation. Analyzing distributed system research proposals reveals design techniques and primitives that do and do not work well when designing practical and deployable system architectures. Since theoretically secure systems that are hard to use or have high performance costs will not be practically accepted, I wish to explore software and systems that provide practical and usable security.
